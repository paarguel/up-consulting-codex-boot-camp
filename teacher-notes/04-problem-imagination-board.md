# Teacher Notes 04 - Problem Imagination Board

## Goal

Help the learner discover useful Codex tasks from real office frustration.

Most beginners do not know what to ask AI to do. Do not start with an empty brainstorm. Start with categories.

## Whiteboard title

```text
Where does your work get messy?
```

## Columns

```text
1. Repetitive work
2. Messy inputs
3. Writing work
4. Spreadsheet or list work
5. Planning work
6. Follow-up work
7. Reporting work
```

## Prompts for the learner

Ask:

- What do you rewrite often?
- What arrives messy?
- What list do you not trust?
- What meeting never turns into action?
- What report takes too long?
- Who are you always waiting on?
- What process only lives in someone's head?

## Convert frustration into workflow

Use this frame:

```text
Input -> Desired Output -> Review Risk -> Codex Workflow
```

Example:

```text
Input:
Messy notes, old announcement, volunteer list, budget

Desired Output:
Event command center and parent email draft

Review Risk:
Dates, times, child safety, costs, room reservations

Codex Workflow:
Event command center prompt plus final-send checklist
```

## Sticky-note examples

```text
I rewrite the same announcement every week.
I forget who I am waiting on.
I get rosters with missing information.
Meeting notes never turn into action.
I have to make reports from scattered emails.
I do not know what to prioritize.
Our process only lives in one person's head.
```

## Close the exercise

Ask the learner to choose one real workflow to map, but do not use real sensitive data during the boot camp.

Teaching line:

> We are not asking "What can AI do?" We are asking "Where does my work turn messy, repetitive, or hard to review?"
